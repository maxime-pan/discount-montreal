Discount Montréal – Usability Study

SOEN 357 · Team 123 · Concordia University · Winter 2026

Document 1: Participant Information Sheet & Consent Form

About This Study

We are a group of four Concordia students working on a course project for SOEN 357 (User Interface Design). We designed a mobile app called Discount Montréal that helps Montreal residents find grocery deals more easily, and we need your help to test it.

This study compares two interfaces: a traditional grocery flyer app and our new design. We want to see which one is easier and faster to use for common grocery-shopping tasks.

What You Will Be Asked to Do

  • Use two different app interfaces to complete three short grocery-related tasks
  • Think out loud while you work so we can understand your thought process
  • Answer a short questionnaire at the end (about 5 minutes)
  • Total estimated time: 15–20 minutes

Your Rights as a Participant

  • Participation is completely voluntary — you can stop at any time, no questions asked
  • No personal identifying information will be collected or stored
  • All data is anonymized and will only be used for this academic course project
  • You will not be judged on how well you perform — we are testing the app, not you
This study is conducted for academic purposes only as part of a university course. The data will not be published or shared outside of the course submission.

Document 2: Task Instructions

Instructions for the Participant

You will complete the following three tasks using each of the two interfaces — once on Interface A and once on Interface B. The order will be assigned to you by the researcher.

Please read each task carefully before starting. As you work, think aloud — tell us what you are looking at, what you are trying to do, and what is confusing or easy. There are no right or wrong ways to approach this. We are testing the interface, not you.

The researcher will time each task from when you say "ready" until you say "done." Please try to complete tasks as quickly and accurately as you can, but do not rush so much that you make mistakes.

Task 1 – Find cheapest milk

You need to buy a 2-litre carton of 2% milk. Find the cheapest option available at a store near your current location. Tell the researcher: the store name, the price, and how far away it is.

Correct answer: Maxi — $3.82 — 1.4 km (with 15% discount applied)

Task 2 – Find prices for eggs and bread

You need to buy a dozen eggs and a loaf of whole wheat bread. Find the best price for each item at any nearby store. Tell the researcher the price and store for each.

Correct answer: Eggs → Super C $4.64 (5% off) · Bread → Maxi $3.19

Task 3 – Best deal for a small shopping list

You need to buy milk, eggs, and tomatoes. Find the best overall deal for these three items — either at one store or split across stores if that saves more money. Tell the researcher which store(s) you would choose and why.

Correct answer: Best single-store option is Maxi (milk $3.82 + eggs $5.29 + tomatoes $2.51)

After Both Interfaces

Once you have completed all three tasks on both interfaces, the researcher will give you a short questionnaire. Please answer honestly based on your experience.
